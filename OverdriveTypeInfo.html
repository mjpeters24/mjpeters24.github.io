Difference's In Overdrive, What are They?

What is Overdrive?

Overdrive is a guitar effect that simulates a tube amp being pushed so it breaks up a little—more grit, sustain, and bite without full-on distortion. With pedals, you mostly see two flavors: Non-transparent overdrive, which boosts the mids and tightens the low end so you cut through a mix; And transparent overdrive, which keeps your guitar and amp’s natural tone but just adds breakup and a bit of push. Both do “drive,” they just affect your sound differently.

What are the differences?

Transparent overdrive aims to keep your guitar and amp’s natural voice intact that being, flat(ish) EQ, lighter compression, and touch-sensitive breakup that mostly adds level and soft clipping. Non-transparent overdrive imposes its own tone: a shaped EQ (often a mid hump or some bass roll-off), more compression, and a distinct clipping character that tightens lows and helps you cut through a mix. In short, transparent = preserves; non-transparent = colors.
